Audio samples from "CONDITIONAL LEARNING WITH LIGHTWEIGHT PROSODIC-LINGUISTIC FEATURES"

Authors: Mahsa Elyasi, Gaurav Bharaj

Abstract: Neural sequence-to-sequence text-to-speech synthesis (TTS) methods such as Tacotron-2 [1] transform text into high-quality speech. However, generating speech with more naturalprosody still remains a challenge. Yasuda et al. [2] show thatTacotron-2’s encoder does not fully model prosodic featuresfrom characters, and results in flat fundamental frequency (F0) variations.

We propose a novel strategy of conditioning (English) Tacotron-2 on lightweight prosodic features – lexicalstress and pitch accent. These features are conditionally applied at three stages of Tacotron-2’s architecture: pre-encoder,post-encoder, and intra-decoder. Here, jointly conditioned features at pre-encoder and intra-decoder stages result in prosodically natural synthesized speech vs. Tacotron-2-based baselines.

Quantitative evaluations show that our formulation achieves higher F0 correlation, and lower Mel Cepstral Distortion measure between synthesized and natural speech. Subjective evaluations show that the proposed method’s Mean Opinion Score of 4.084 fairs higher than baselines (3.704,3.808, 3.867), with natural speech at 4.28.
